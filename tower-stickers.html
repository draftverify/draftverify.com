Identification

Tower Stickers (Point-of-Service Identification)

Tower stickers are the guest-visible mark that a faucet dispenses a non-alcoholic product. This page provides practical guidance for installers and venue teams to select materials, place labels correctly, and maintain readability in cold, humid, and high-volume environments. DraftVerify publishes the specification; venues implement and self-check against these guidelines.

Instant Recognition

High-contrast wording and simple iconography make non-alcoholic faucets unambiguous for staff and guests.

Service-Ready

Cold-room-rated adhesive, chemical-resistant laminate, and curved-surface compliance minimize lift and peeling.

Recordkeeping

Optional serialization or QR helps venues link placements to their internal compliance records and cleaning logs.

Specification

Color, Typography & Iconography

Primary Mark

NON-ALCOHOLIC in uppercase using the DraftVerify palette: Gold (background) / Blue (text & keyline).

  • Contrast ratio ≥ 7:1 (accessible in low light)
  • Finish: matte, non-glare
  • Edge: blue keyline for definition on stainless/chrome

Typography

Humanist sans (system-safe). Designed for readability at 40–60 cm working distance.

  • Cap height ≥ 3.8 mm on small label
  • Cap height ≥ 5.0 mm on standard label

Iconography

Simple “NA” lockup or NA-drop icon. Optional sub-labels to clarify category:

  • NA Beer (default)
  • Kombucha — acid-compatible line
  • Nitro Coffee — nitrogen service
Engineering

Materials, Adhesion & Service Conditions

Label Stock

Conformable polymer film with cold-flow adhesive; suitable for curved towers and shanks. Working temp −2°C to 40°C (28–104°F).

Lamination

Matte overlaminate with resistance to typical bar cleaners; anti-scuff for high-touch areas; non-yellowing.

Adhesion

Permanent PSA, 24-hour full cure. Surface prep is required on stainless, chrome, and powder-coat finishes.

Placement

Operator-Facing & Guest-Facing Placement

The NA mark should be clearly visible to the operator during every pour. Where practical, provide a secondary guest-visible mark. Labels must not obstruct cleaning, tap handles, or drip tray access.

Operator-Facing

  • Place on tower face or faucet shank within 80–150 mm of the faucet
  • Align to faucet; avoid handle movement arcs
  • Maintain 4–6 mm clear margin at label edges

Guest-Facing (Optional)

  • Mirror label on guest side if tower geometry allows
  • Avoid areas with constant condensation pooling
  • Do not conflict with menu boards or brand marks

Clearance & Curvature

  • Minimum curve radius ≥ 35 mm for standard size
  • Use small format on tighter radii
  • Do not bridge seams, screws, or sharp transitions
Formats

Available Sizes

Small

35 × 12 mm (shanks, tight radii). Maintains minimum legibility at short distances.

Standard

48 × 16 mm (most towers). Recommended default for operator-facing placement.

Wide

60 × 18 mm (flat tower panels). Good for guest-facing or high-contrast installs.

Procedure

Installation Steps (Venue-Led)

  1. Confirm the faucet dispenses a non-alcoholic line (check coupler tag, seal, and venue record).
  2. Prepare surface: clean, rinse, and dry. Remove oils/condensation; bring to room temperature if possible.
  3. Select size based on curvature; dry-fit before removing the liner.
  4. Apply from center out with firm pressure; avoid stretching; keep 4–6 mm edge clearance.
  5. Optionally record the placement and (if used) serialization/QR in the venue’s internal record; photograph for reference.
  6. Allow adhesive to cure (up to 24 hours) before aggressive cleaning.

Maintenance

Cleaning Compatibility & Replacement

Chemicals

Compatible with typical bar cleaners and sanitizer residues. Avoid prolonged exposure to strong solvents or abrasives.

Self-Check Interval

Venue-led visual check weekly; confirm legibility at each line clean. Replace if lifting, scuffed, or unreadable in service lighting.

Replacement Events

  • Reorientation or tap reassignment
  • Condensation damage or chemical etching
  • Annual refresh alongside internal verification review
Self-Check

Venue Checklist

Identification

  • Operator-facing sticker present and legible
  • Guest-facing sticker used where practical
  • Placement within 80–150 mm of faucet

Condition

  • No lift, bubbling, or edge peel
  • Matte surface; text remains clear in low light
  • No interference with handle movement

Records (Optional)

  • Photo on file for each faucet
  • If used, QR/serial noted in venue record
  • Date of last replacement and next review
