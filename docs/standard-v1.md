## 1. Purpose, Scope, and Status

### **1.1 Purpose**
The purpose of this Standard is to define a **uniform, auditable, and globally consistent**  
framework for:

- **Identifying** NA draft beverages  
- **Separating** NA pathways from alcoholic pathways  
- **Verifying** NA draft integrity using physical and digital controls  

The Standard ensures NA draft service is:

- safe  
- repeatable  
- demonstrably compliant  
- trusted by operators and consumers  

### **1.2 Scope**
This Standard applies to all draft-dispensed beverages meeting the legal  
definition of non-alcoholic within the applicable jurisdiction.

It is applicable to:

- breweries producing NA draft products  
- distributors handling NA draft products  
- venues serving NA draft products  
- mixed systems and dedicated NA systems  
- DraftVerify partners and certified operators  

This Standard governs:

- identification systems  
- tagging requirements  
- digital activation  
- verification routines  
- recordkeeping  
- incident response  
- compliance and mark usage  

### **1.3 Status**
DraftVerify Standard v1.0 is a **voluntary, industry-accepted standard**.  
It does **not** replace federal, provincial/state, or municipal regulatory  
requirements. When conflicts occur, **regulatory law overrides**.

The most current version is always available at:  
**https://www.draftverify.com/standards/**

## 3. System Architecture

### **3.1 Dual-Layer Compliance Model**
Compliance requires **both**:

1. **Physical Identification Layer**  
2. **Digital Activation Layer**

Physical identity without digital activation is **not compliant**.  
Digital activation without visible identification is **not compliant**.

### **3.2 Continuous Identity Chain**
Each NA draft pathway must maintain a continuous identity through:

- keg  
- coupler  
- line  
- faucet  

Breaks in physical identity or digital records invalidate compliance.

### **3.3 Identification Components**
Identification elements may include:

- keg collar  
- coupler tag  
- line identity tags  
- faucet markers  

Specifications are detailed in **ID-Series Standards (ID-1 through ID-5).**

## 4. Core Requirements

### **4.1 NA Separation**
Operators must:

- maintain isolation between NA and alcoholic draft pathways  
- follow the Product Switching & Line Reuse Protocol for any shared components  
- comply with **Tower Sharing Standards** where applicable  

### **4.2 Physical Identification**
All NA components must be:

- visible from the operator‚Äôs working distance  
- durable in cold-room and high-humidity environments  
- correctly placed per ID-Series specifications  
- consistent at all points along the NA pathway  

### **4.3 Digital Activation**
All serialized tags must be:

- activated in the DraftVerify Registry  
- assigned to the correct component  
- updated upon system modification  

Tags are valid only when **Active**, **correctly linked**, and **digitally verifiable**.

### **4.4 Verification & Records**
Operators must maintain:

- verification logs  
- cleaning logs  
- change logs  
- incident reports  

Upstream partners (breweries, distributors) must maintain corresponding logs.

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

### **5.1 Breweries**
Responsible for:

- correct keg tagging  
- serialization per F-12  
- activation per F-13  
- maintaining batch documentation  
- communicating NA-specific handling requirements  

### **5.2 Distributors**
Responsible for:

- NA segregation during transport  
- cold chain preservation  
- exception handling documentation  
- maintaining chain-of-custody  

### **5.3 Venues**
Responsible for:

- installing NA identification  
- completing first verification (F-18)  
- conducting ongoing checks  
- training staff on NA service  
- implementing corrective actions  

### **5.4 DraftVerify Initiative**
Responsible for:

- maintaining this Standard  
- publishing revisions  
- operating the digital Registry  
- providing guidance and education  

DraftVerify is **not** a regulatory enforcement body.

## 6. Verification, Compliance, and Certification Mark

### **6.1 Verification Levels**
Operators may be recognized as:

- **Self-Verified**  
- **Brewery-Verified**  
- **DraftVerify-Verified**  

### **6.2 Use of Certification Mark**
The DraftVerify mark may be used only when:

- all Core Requirements are met  
- all required logs are maintained  
- audits are passed (where applicable)  

Misuse is subject to enforcement under the **Certification & Mark Use Policy (F-32).**

## 7. Incident Response & Continuous Improvement

### **7.1 Mis-Serve or Cross-Contact**
If an incident occurs:

- affected components must be immediately locked out  
- incident must be logged in the Registry  
- root cause analysis conducted  
- corrective action implemented  

NA service may resume only after compliance is restored.

### **7.2 Field Findings**
Operators and partners may submit findings that contribute to future revisions.
