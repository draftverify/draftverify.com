<!-- CORE RISKS -->
<section class="section" id="core-risks">
  <div class="container">
    <h2 class="h2 center">The Risks DraftVerify Is Designed to Remove</h2>
    <p class="std-section-intro">
      NA draft risk clusters around three failure points: <strong>accidental alcohol service</strong>,
      <strong>mis-connections in the cooler</strong>, and <strong>contamination or mishandling</strong>.
      Each of these can be traced back to unclear identification and a lack of verification.
    </p>

    <div class="std-series-grid">

      <div class="std-series-card">
        <div class="std-series-label">Risk 1</div>
        <h3>Accidental Alcohol Service</h3>
        <p>
          A guest who orders NA may be driving, pregnant, on medication, or in recovery. Serving alcohol by
          mistake is not a minor error; it is a high-impact event for the guest and the venue.
        </p>
        <ul>
          <li>Identical-looking taps create confusion during busy service.</li>
          <li>New staff may not know which handle actually pours NA.</li>
          <li>DIY labels fade, peel, or are removed when towers are rebranded.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="std-series-card">
        <div class="std-series-label">Risk 2</div>
        <h3>Mis-Connections in the Cooler</h3>
        <p>
          Coolers are high-change environments. Kegs rotate, couplers move, and lines get reused. Without a
          clear identification standard, the NA path can be broken without anyone noticing.
        </p>
        <ul>
          <li>NA kegs connected to alcoholic lines, or vice versa.</li>
          <li>Faucets pouring a different product than the badge implies.</li>
          <li>No simple way to confirm the full path from keg → faucet.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="std-series-card">
        <div class="std-series-label">Risk 3</div>
        <h3>Contamination &amp; Hygiene</h3>
        <p>
          NA products often behave differently in draft systems than traditional beer. When NA lines are not
          clearly separated or properly documented, contamination and handling issues become harder to control.
        </p>
        <ul>
          <li>Unclear which lines have run NA vs. alcoholic products.</li>
          <li>Cleaning schedules can miss lines used for NA.</li>
          <li>Breweries lose visibility into how their NA product is treated.</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

    </div>
  </div>
</section>
