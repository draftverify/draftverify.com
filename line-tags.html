Identification

Line Tags & Tamper Seals

DraftVerify line tags make non-alcoholic (NA) lines unmistakable at every touchpoint. Color coding, serialized IDs, and tamper-evident seals turn visual identification into a documented control: preventing cross-contact, accelerating inspections, and reinforcing training across shifts and venues.

Clarity

High-contrast colors and plain-language labels remove ambiguity for staff and guests—even in low light or high volume service.

Consistency

Standard placement at coupler → trunk/line → tower/faucet maintains a continuous identification path from keg to glass.

Compliance

Serialized tags, seal numbers, and photo logs link back to the venue’s DraftVerify record for audits and renewals.

Specification

Color & Labeling System

Primary NA Identifier

Gold/Blue (DraftVerify) with “NON-ALCOHOLIC” in uppercase. Minimum contrast ratio 7:1 for accessibility.

  • Background: Gold (DNV-G)
  • Text/Edge: Blue (DNV-B)
  • Finish: Matte, non-glare

Secondary Codes

Optional sub-tags for category specificity:

  • NA Beer (default)
  • Kombucha — Acid-compatible line
  • Nitro Coffee — Nitrogen service

Serialization

Each kit ships with unique IDs:

  • Tag ID (TT-####-YYYY)
  • Seal ID (TS-####-YYYY)
  • Optional QR linking to the venue record
Engineering

Materials & Durability

Tags

Polymer or coated aluminum; rounded corners; chemical-resistant inks. Temperature range −2°C to 40°C (28°F–104°F).

Seals

Frangible, tamper-evident seals rated for cold storage and moisture; adhesive compatible with stainless and chrome.

Mounting

Food-service cable ties or approved clips. No sharp edges; no interference with cleaning or coupler operation.

Placement

Coupler → Line → Tower

Identification must be continuous along the flow path. For NA beer, place all three identifiers. For secondary categories, follow the same pattern with the appropriate sub-tag.

1) Coupler

Primary tag secured to the coupler body; seal bridges the coupler collar. Label faces outward and remains visible with keg in place.

2) Trunk/Line

Inline tag every 1.5–2 m on accessible runs, or at entry/exit points of insulated trunks. No contact with moving parts.

3) Tower/Faucet

Sticker at the tower face and/or faucet shank. Must be readable to the operator and visible to guests where practical.

Procedure

Installation Steps (Field)

  1. Confirm the beverage category and gas routing for each line; isolate NA circuits from alcoholic manifolds.
  2. Clean and dry mounting surfaces; remove oils and condensation at towers and couplers.
  3. Apply coupler tag + seal; record Seal ID on the venue record.
  4. Apply line tags along accessible runs; avoid bends tighter than manufacturer spec.
  5. Apply tower/faucet sticker facing operator/guest; verify readability in service lighting.
  6. Photograph each placement and submit to the DraftVerify venue record; verify serialization continuity.
  7. Brief staff using the quick guide; log who received training and where the guide is posted.

Inspection

Audit Checklist (On-Site)

Separation

  • NA lines on dedicated gas rails
  • No shared couplers/fittings with alcoholic systems
  • Backflow/check-valve configuration verified

Identification

  • Coupler, line, and tower all tagged
  • Tags readable in service lighting
  • Seal and tag IDs match the venue record

Documentation

  • Photo log completed
  • Cleaning cadence recorded
  • Operator briefing documented
Maintenance

Replacement & Care

When to Replace

  • Seal broken or removed
  • Tag unreadable, torn, or contaminated
  • System re-routed or new trunk installed

Intervals

Visual check weekly; formal tag/seal review with each line clean; full re-issue on system change or annual renewal.

Storage

Store kits in a dry, room-temperature location. Do not freeze. Keep serialization cards with venue records.

FAQ

Common Questions

Can tags be used on shared towers?

Yes, but NA faucets must have tower-face stickers and lock-out identification. Gas and line separation are still required.

Do tags replace cleaning logs?

No. Tags are an identification control. Cleaning cadence and sanitizer validation remain separate documented controls.

What if a seal breaks during service?

Replace the seal immediately, photograph the repair, and update the venue record with the new Seal ID and reason.

Are QR codes required?

Recommended for high-volume venues and multi-site programs. QR codes accelerate spot checks and digital record retrieval.
